Olumo Rock

Olumo Rock is a mountain in Abeokuta, Ogun State, Nigeria, that served as a natural fortress for the Egba people during inter-tribal wars in the 19th century. Rising about 137 meters above sea level, the rock offers panoramic views of the city. Olumo Rock features several natural caves, some of which are large enough to accommodate up to 100 people. Today, it is a popular tourist destination with facilities such as a museum, a restaurant, and an observation platform.

River Niger-Benue

The River Niger and River Benue are the two largest rivers in Nigeria and play crucial roles in the country's geography and economy. The River Niger is the principal river of western Africa, running through several countries before entering Nigeria and flowing into the Atlantic Ocean. The River Benue is the major tributary of the Niger, joining it at Lokoja, Kogi State. The confluence of these rivers is a significant geographical landmark and supports agriculture, fishing, and transportation in the region. The rivers are also vital for hydroelectric power generation, with several dams constructed along their courses.

Zuma Rock

Zuma Rock is a large monolith located in Niger State, Nigeria, just north of the capital city, Abuja. Standing at about 725 meters above its surroundings, Zuma Rock is often referred to as the "Gateway to Abuja" due to its prominent position near the main highway leading into the city. The rock is noted for its distinct shape and the human face-like features visible on its surface. Zuma Rock holds cultural significance and is an iconic symbol of Nigeria.

Lekki Conservation Centre
